≑ About
I am an engineer interested in systems/information theory, logistics/energy infrastructure, experience design, athletic performance, and music theory.

I am a firm believer in individual agency and in taste/conviction as the sine qua non for building a beautiful future.

Trained in atoms (mechanical engineering), I am leveling up in the craft of bits. I believe that technology must be inconspicuous, like electricity, and cyclically positive, like the bicycle. I enjoy good design and products like Moka, FlightRadar24, and Are.na - especially those that trigger strong emotional responses.

Once a VC analyst, I could no longer stand to watch the game from the sidelines. I believe that the life of a food critic who does not know how to cook is not a life worth living.

I am currently spending my time thinking about problems in logistics, cultivating the habit of building product, and letting my curiosity run wild.

Currently in Lisbon.
Formerly in Rio de Janeiro, São Paulo, Philadelphia, New York City.

≑ Moving
I've come to appreciate running and long-walking as tools for exploration, mindfulness, and connection to city life. I am currently training for the Comrades Marathon (90km or 55mi, from Pietermaritzburg to Durban) and hope to break 2:30 in the marathon in 2021. There's something religiously appealing about Ultra Trail Mt. Fuji and Marathon des Sables. They remain - for now - a mere disturbance in the force...

≑ Performing
I like to play the piano. My favorite composers are Rachmaninoff, Ravel, and Chopin. I believe that Grigory Sokolov is unequaled in skill, clarity, and often in sensibility as a performer. Russian classical music puzzles me - I can't seem to understand my feelings. I'm currently working on Poulenc's Improvisation #7 and my ultimate goal is to tackle Chopin's Scherzo No.2 (Op. 31) and Ravel's Miroirs III: Une Barque sur L'Océan.

≑ Cooking
I enjoy the frustrations and exhilarations of cooking. I went down the culinary rabbit hole when Harold McGee told me that searing meat does not seal in its juices. I never came back. Lloyd Morse (no-bullshit, resilient) and LEV (site-specific, single-ingredient) embody how I approach food and cookery. I am drawn towards cuisines with strong flavors including Indian, Lebanese, Mexican, Ethiopian, and Japanese.
