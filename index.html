≑ About
I am a former VC analyst who could no longer stand to watch the game from the sidelines. I believe (naively?) that the life of a food critic who does not know how to cook is not a life worth living.

I'm taking time off to think about problems in education, logistics, and hospitaly:
≑ The engineering curriculum fosters deductive rather than inductive thinking.
≑ Freight forwarders are stuck in the past.
≑ Restaurants have the least resilient of business models.

Trained in atoms (mechanical engineering), I am currently leveling up my craft with bits. I believe that technology must be inconspicuous, like electricity, and cyclically positive, like the bycicle. I enjoy good design and products like Moka, FlightRadar24, and Are.na.

≑ Eggplant whispering
I like to cook. I went down the culinary rabbit hole when Harold McGee told me that searing meat does not seal in its juices. I never came back. Lloyd Morse (no-bullshit, resilient) and LEV (site-specific, single-ingredient) embody how I approach food and cookery. Eggplants are underrated and here's my cooking playlist.

≑ Walking
I like to walk, empty-minded. Walking attenuates my compulsive moving habits (running, cycling, swimming, surfing, and kitesurfing). I once thought I could make the Portuguese marathon team in the 2024 Olympics. Injuries and the IAAF kept me humble.

≑ Reveries
I like to play the piano. I believe that Grigory Sokolov is unequaled in skill, clarity, and often in sensibility. I'm currently stuck on Poulenc's Improvisation #7.
